# Mercier Broome Leeper Family Trust — Integration Addendum

## For Corporate Structure + Business Banking Alignment (Separate, End-to-End Update)

> This addendum is a structured integration companion to the Family Trust document you provided.  
> It is designed to keep trust governance separate while enabling practical alignment with LBWG/MERCDEE operating and banking workflows.

---

## 1) Purpose of This Addendum

This addendum establishes a practical integration framework so the **Mercier Broome Leeper Family Trust** can:

1. Interface cleanly with business-banking operations.
2. Support trustee-controlled funding, reserve policy, and beneficiary-safe distributions.
3. Align with entity-level corporate and banking controls without collapsing legal separateness.

---

## 2) Governance Separation and Role Clarity

### A. Separation Principles

- The Family Trust remains a separate legal arrangement from corporate entities.
- Corporate debts/obligations are not automatically trust obligations unless expressly authorized.
- Trust assets must be administered under trustee fiduciary duties and trust terms.

### B. Role Mapping

- **Grantor:** As identified in trust instrument.
- **Trustee/Successor Trustee(s):** As named in trust instrument.
- **Entity Officers/Directors (LBWG/MERCDEE):** Separate authority; may not direct trust distributions unless legally authorized and documented.

### C. Conflict Rule

If a corporate instruction conflicts with trust terms, the Trustee follows trust terms first.

---

## 3) Banking Integration Model (Trust + Operating Entities)

### A. Account Topology

Recommended separate account stack:

1. **Family Trust Primary Account** (trust receipts/disbursements)
2. **Family Trust Reserve Account** (tax/liquidity/special-purpose reserve)
3. **Entity Operating Accounts** (LBWG and MERCDEE remain separate)

### B. Transfer Controls

- No commingling between trust and entity funds.
- Any transfer between trust and entity accounts requires:
  - Written purpose memo,
  - Trustee approval,
  - Supporting legal/accounting rationale,
  - Retention in trust administration records.

### C. Signer/Approval Matrix

- Trust accounts: Trustee-controlled approvals.
- Entity accounts: Board/officer approvals per entity banking resolutions.
- Dual approval for transfers above threshold (e.g., $5,000 or policy-defined amount).

---

## 4) Trust-to-Entity Funding Decision Framework

Before any trust-to-entity transfer:

1. Confirm transfer is consistent with trust terms and fiduciary duty.
2. Document transfer type:
   - Loan,
   - Capital support,
   - Reimbursement,
   - Expense advance.
3. Obtain legal/tax review if non-routine.
4. Capture repayment terms (if loan) and bookkeeping treatment.

---

## 5) Beneficiary and Distribution Protections

Based on trust language regarding minors/incompetency and support:

- Maintain beneficiary-specific ledgers for restricted distributions.
- Ensure institutional/public-benefit-sensitive handling remains compliant with trust terms.
- Do not route beneficiary-directed funds through entity operating accounts.

---

## 6) Standardized Operating Controls (End-to-End)

### A. Monthly Controls

- Bank reconciliations for trust and each entity account.
- Variance and unusual-activity review.
- Ledger tie-out for trust distributions and inter-account movements.

### B. Quarterly Controls

- Signer and access review.
- Trustee oversight memo.
- Legal/compliance review for cross-entity transactions.

### C. Annual Controls

- Year-end trust accounting package.
- Tax package prep support (trust + entities, separately).
- Banking relationship performance and fee analysis.

---

## 7) Required Records Packet (Trust Integration)

Keep these records together:

- Family Trust instrument + amendments.
- Trustee acceptance/succession records.
- Banking resolutions/authorizations (trust-specific).
- Transfer memos (trust <-> entity, if any).
- Distribution justifications.
- Reconciliations and year-end accounting files.

---

## 8) Suggested Banking SOP Clauses (Trust Context)

1. **No Commingling Clause**  
   Trust funds and entity funds must never be mixed in one account.

2. **Purpose-Locked Transfer Clause**  
   Every outbound trust transfer must include a written purpose and approval record.

3. **Escalation Clause**  
   If uncertain, Trustee escalates to legal counsel before execution.

4. **Audit-Ready Clause**  
   All approvals, statements, and transfer support retained for audit/legal review.
